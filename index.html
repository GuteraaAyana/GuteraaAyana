Projects

Soccer Analytics Research: Goal-Scoring Pattern Analysis

Problem: Analyzed goal-scoring patterns across 21 professional soccer leagues to identify temporal trends and tactical shifts in the sport.

Solution: Built a regression-based statistical model using Python to process and analyze thousands of matches. Created automated data pipelines with SLURM for large-scale computation and generated visualizations revealing significant patterns in goal timing and frequency.

Key Findings: Discovered that goal-scoring probability increases as matches progress, with significantly fewer goals than expected in the early minutes of each half (χ² ≈ 288.62, p = 3.72 × 10⁻²¹). Identified "bursty" goal-scoring behavior where teams are more likely to score consecutive goals shortly after their previous goal, revealing temporal clustering patterns that deviate from random expectation. Analysis of 3,433 matches across 21 leagues demonstrates that goal timing is context-dependent, influenced by factors like fatigue, tactical shifts, and psychological momentum.

Technologies: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib), SLURM, Git, Statistical Regression Modeling

Impact: Research published as a pre-print on arXiv, demonstrating reproducible methodology for sports performance analysis.
